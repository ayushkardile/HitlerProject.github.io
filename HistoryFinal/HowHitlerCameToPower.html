How He Came To Power

A common misconception about Hitler's rise to power was that Hitler did not seize power in a "Coupe" and that the Nazi party was not directly elected into power. Hitler and the Nazi party came to power in Germany's legal political processes. The Nazi Party was not always popular and powerful. Before this party came to fame it was small, ineffective, and marginal. In 1920 the Nazi party was one of many new Radical Movements in Germany. Led by Adolf Hitler this party tried and failed to violently seize power. This failed Coup is known as the Beer Hall Putsch. After this failure, the Nazi party, in the mid-1920's tried a more political approach but only received the votes of 3% of the national population. However in the beginning of the 1930's the Nazi party started to win more votes, this was largely caused by the economic crisis in Germany. In the mid-1930s, the Nazi party continued to gain more support until one election they won over 37% of the overall votes in Germany which was the most a single party had at the time winning an election. After winning the elections Hitler refused to work with any other party and demanded the chancellor to appoint him as chancellor, and on January 30, 1933, Adolf Hitler was appointed chancellor of Germany by German President Paul von Hindenburg.
